About the Project

This site is an interactive tool that prompts users to consider some questions about maps– what they should look like, what information they can convey, and how they reflect power and culture.

The homepage consists of a survey that asks users to select between types of representation, demonstrating how changing even a few parameters can produce radically different maps of the same region.

We use the region of Anatolia as our case study. This choice was made for two reasons: first, in order to focus users’ attention on a particular area and thus make it easier to identify similarities and differences between maps; second, to provide a counterpoint with the second section of the website. This second component continues to explore the issues raised by the homepage by further narrowing the focus, now considering different approaches to mapping Hittite Anatolia.
